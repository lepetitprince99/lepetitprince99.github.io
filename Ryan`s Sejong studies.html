Sejong University is a private university in Seoul, South Korea. It was founded in 1940 and is named after King Sejong the Great, who is known for creating the Korean alphabet, Hangul. The university offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs in various fields, including humanities, social sciences, natural sciences, engineering, and arts. Sejong University is also known for its strong emphasis on research and has a number of research centers and institutes dedicated to various fields of study.
